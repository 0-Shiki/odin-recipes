Chicken Thighs in Pan Sauce

Description

This is a fairly simple recipe of my own design. It's pretty easy to make and doesn't require many ingredients. Best done in a stainless steel pan.

Ingredients

  • chicken thighs (preferably boneless but skin-on)
  • neutral cooking oil
  • mirin
  • soy sauce
  • half a shallot, diced
  • rosemary
  • a few tablespoons of butter
  • salt

Steps

  1. If the chicken thighs are bone-in, remove the bone.
  2. Preheat the pan on medium to medium-high heat, then coat the bottom in your neutral cooking oil.
  3. Fry the chicken thighs until both sides are golden brown. As they fry, dice the shallot.
  4. Take the chicken thighs out and deglaze the pan with mirin, making sure to scrape up the fond. Add a splash of soy sauce and the diced shallot. Saute the shallot in the sauce until golden brown, then take off the heat.
  5. Once the sauce stops bubbling, add the butter and rosemary and incorporate until the sauce is creamy. Pour the sauce over the chicken and serve.
